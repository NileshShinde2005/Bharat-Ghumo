Kudroli Gokarnanath Temple

Overview

Kudroli Gokarnanath Temple is a renowned Hindu temple in Mangalore, Karnataka, dedicated to Lord Shiva. Built in 1912 by Narayana Guru for the Billava community, the temple is a magnificent example of modern Dravidian architecture with golden domes, vibrant walls, and a tranquil atmosphere.

Highlights

  • Golden Gopurams and intricately carved architecture
  • Illuminated temple complex during Mangalore Dasara
  • Massive golden chariots used in festival processions
  • Idols of Ganapati, Subramanya, Annapoorneshwari, and more

Festivals

The temple is especially famous for its grand celebration of Mangalore Dasara, which features dazzling lights, cultural performances, and religious rituals, attracting thousands of devotees and tourists every year.

History

The temple was founded by Shri Narayana Guru, a revered social reformer, to allow members of the Billava community full access to spiritual practices. It has since grown into a powerful symbol of equality and devotion.
